The creed

Five things I believe
before we talk.

If any of these feel wrong, we shouldn't work together. That's the point of writing them down.

01 / THE CREED
Specialists ship specialty. The business problem sits between their lanes.
Growth dies in the gaps — between marketing and product, between strategy and the stack. I work in the gaps on purpose.
02 / THE CREED
Start from the business outcome. Every time. No exceptions.
Not from the channel. Not from the tool. Not from what I happen to enjoy building this quarter. The outcome first — and then the shortest honest path to it.
03 / THE CREED
No hours sold. No reports filed. Value created. Compounding.
A retainer buys the judgement, not the timesheet. I work in systems that keep working after the engagement ends.
04 / THE CREED
Depth beats surface advice — every single time.
I'd rather know one thing all the way down than ten things well enough to nod at. Mastery is knowing what you're allowed to skip.
05 / THE CREED
One client at a time. That's the only way the math works.
Full attention is the product. If I'm splitting it across five logos, I'm selling a worse version of the thing you hired me for.

Operating principles

How the work actually runs.

The rules I wrote for myself after the third engagement where I should have said something sooner.

PRINCIPLE / 01

Outcomes, not outputs.

A PDF is not a result. A meeting is not a result. A system that moves a number — that's a result. Everything else is scaffolding.

PRINCIPLE / 02

Ship production, not pilots.

Five-plus AI-powered products into production. Not demos, not proofs-of-concept. Real things that real people use on a Tuesday.

PRINCIPLE / 03

Write it down, or it didn't happen.

Every decision has a paper trail. Every system has a runbook. The work outlasts the engagement because the thinking is legible.

PRINCIPLE / 04

Disagree in writing, early.

The cost of a direct objection in week one is always lower than the cost of a polite shrug in month three. I'll tell you when I think you're wrong.

PRINCIPLE / 05

Automate the boring half.

If a human is doing it twice, a script should do it the third time. AI is a leverage tool, not a headline — it earns its place by disappearing.

PRINCIPLE / 06

Stop when it's stopped working.

Retainers end when the compounding stops. I'll tell you before you have to ask. That's the only way this stays honest.
